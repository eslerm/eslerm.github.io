Vietnamese Pork Chops

Yields: 4 servings

Ingredients:
  • Shallot, 1 small
  • Light brown sugar, 1/3 cup
  • Fish sauce, 1/4 cup
  • Rice Vinegar, 2 tbsp
  • Black Pepper, 1 tsp
  • Pork chops, 4 1/2" thick-cut
  • Vegetable oil, 1 tbsp
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Lime halves, for serving
Steps:
  1. Whisk shallot, brown sugar, fish sauce, vinegar, and pepper in a small bowl. Pour in zip lock with pork chops to marinate overnight.
  2. Grab a large skillet and a small saucepan. Remove pork chops from marinade and scrape excess marinade into the saucepan. Heat oil in large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook pork chops until browned and internal temperature reaches 145F, about 4 minutes per side. Let pork chops rest 10 minutes before serving.
  3. Meanwhile, pour rest of marinade into saucepan and bring to boil until it has reduced to 1/4 cup, about 4 minutes. Serve pork chops with reduced marinade and lime halves.
