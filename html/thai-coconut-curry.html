Thai Coconut Curry

Yields: 4 servings

Ingredients:
  • Red or green curry paste, 2 oz
  • Vegetable oil, 3 tbsp
  • Onion, 1
  • Garlic, 3 cloves
  • Ginger, 2 inch finger
  • Thai chili pepper, 3
  • Chile Garlic Sauce, spoonful
  • Oyster sauce, 2 tbsp
  • Tamari Sauce, 1 tsp
  • Coconut Milk, 19.5 oz
Steps:
  1. In wok heat oil until hot but, below medium sizzle. Optionally add a few drops of sesame oil and chile infused oil.
  2. Add half can of Maesri curry paste, red or green, and blend it well with oil. "Bhun" the oil-paste by stirring until the oil separates back out of the paste (about 3 minutes). Lower the heat if needed.
  3. Add onions and turn down. Stir for several minutes.
  4. When onions are soft, add garlic, ginger, and peppers. Stir for several more minutes.
  5. Add chile garlic sauce and oyster sauce. Turn up and heat constantly until bubbling. Add tamari sauce.
  6. Add coconut milk and whisk into bhuned paste. Squeeze in the juice of half a lime. Bring to very slow slimmer. Do not boil after adding coconut milk.
  7. Add cilantro. Optionally add stir fried veggies or mushrooms now or add chicken or fish to lightly poach.
Notes:
  • Maesri curry paste and Mae Ploy coconut milk are good brands which can be found at United Noodle in Minneapolis.
  • To store unused half of curry paste can, smooth paste flat and top with oil. Wrap and store in fridge or freezer.
  • Make curry from scratch if possible.
